\section{PMMA Validation Case (MaCFP Benchmark)}

This validation case reproduces the MaCFP benchmark experiment on \textbf{poly(methyl methacrylate) (PMMA)} performed in the NIST Gasification Apparatus under an external radiant heat flux of \(\mathbf{50~\mathrm{kW/m^2}}\).  
The sample consists of a \(\mathbf{5.9~\mathrm{mm}}\) thick PMMA slab placed on a \(\mathbf{12~\mathrm{mm}}\) Kaowool insulation layer, with the back surface fully insulated (adiabatic).  
PMMA is modeled as a non-charring material, decomposing into gas through a single first-order reaction. The kinetic parameters are summarized in Table~\ref{tab:chem_props_pmma}, while the thermophysical properties of PMMA and Kaowool are given in Table~\ref{tab:phys_props_pmma}.  
The simulation uses a uniform spatial discretization of \(\Delta z = 0.025~\mathrm{mm}\) and a time step of \(\Delta t = 0.05~\mathrm{s}\). The results of the simulation are presented in Figure~\ref{fig:MLR_PMMA}.
